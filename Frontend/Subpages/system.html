Human Axial Skeleton

The axial skeleton forms the central axis of the body and includes the bones of the skull, ossicles of the middle ear, hyoid bone of the throat, vertebral column, and the thoracic cage (ribcage) (Figure 29.5). The function of the axial skeleton is to provide support and protection for the brain, the spinal cord, and the organs in the ventral body cavity. It provides a surface for the attachment of muscles that move the head, neck, and trunk, performs respiratory movements, and stabilizes parts of the appendicular skeleton.

Key Points

  • The eight cranial bones are the frontal bone, two parietal bones, two temporal bones, occipital bone, sphenoid bone, and the ethmoid bone.
  • The auditory ossicles of the middle ear transmit sounds from the air as vibrations to the fluid-filled cochlea.
  • The adult vertebral column comprises 26 bones: the 24 vertebrae, the sacrum, and the coccyx bones.

Muscular System

Proximal means closer to the point of attachment or origin, while distal means farther away. For example, the elbow is proximal to the wrist, and the wrist is distal to the elbow.

Superficial describes a position closer to the surface of the body, and deep describes a position farther from the surface. The skin is superficial to the muscles, and the bones are deep to the muscles.
